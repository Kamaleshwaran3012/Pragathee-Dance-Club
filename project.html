Our Classes Description

Bharathanatyam

Bharatanatyam is a classical Indian dance form from Tamil Nadu, known for its grace, rhythmic footwork, and expressive storytelling. It blends three elements: nritta (pure dance), nritya (expressive dance), and natya (dramatic storytelling).

Western Dance

Western dance encompasses a variety of styles originating from Europe and the Americas, including ballet, contemporary, hip-hop, jazz, and more. Each style has its own unique techniques, expressions, and forms of movement. Ballet is known for its structured, graceful movements and emphasis on technique, while contemporary dance allows for more freedom and improvisation. Hip-hop, rooted in urban culture, is energetic and rhythmic.

Modern Dance

Modern dance is a dynamic and expressive form of dance that emerged in the early 20th century as a reaction to the rigid structures of classical ballet. It emphasizes freedom of movement, individuality, and emotional expression. Unlike ballet's formal techniques, modern dance allows dancers to move in more fluid, organic ways, often focusing on the body's natural movements.

Classical Dance

Classical dance refers to traditional and highly structured dance forms that are rooted in the cultural and historical practices of a particular region or country. These forms typically follow strict techniques, movements, and choreography, often with an emphasis on storytelling, mythology, and symbolism. Classical dances are characterized by their discipline, elaborate costumes, and connection to music and rhythm.
